Software/System (Domain-Specific Software):

Domain-specific software is built for a particular field like education, healthcare, or banking. It solves problems unique to that area.

  1. Database Management System (DBMS):

    2. A DBMS stores, manages, and organizes data, making it easy to retrieve, update, and secure information.

  2. Accounting System:

    3. An accounting system records and manages financial transactions like income, expenses, and profits to keep business accounts clear and organized.

  3. Attendance System:

    4. An attendance system tracks when people are present or absent. It's commonly used in schools and offices to manage time and presence records.

Software Development

  • Database Design:

    • It is the process of organizing data into structured tables to store, access, and manage it efficiently and without duplication.

  • Database Administration:

    • It is the management of databases to ensure data is secure, available, backed up, and performing well.
